# 01 — Vision & Goals

## Problem Statement

The funeral industry in Europe remains one of the least digitized sectors. Most funeral homes still rely on phone calls, paper documents, Excel, manual scheduling, fragmented communication, and offline payments.

Customers must often contact multiple organizations separately: cemetery, funeral home, florist, monument company, document services. The process is slow, stressful, and inefficient during an already difficult time.

## Product Vision

**Memora is the operating system for the funeral industry** — not another website builder.

We provide a unified, white-label, multi-tenant platform where every participant (funeral home, cemetery, supplier, customer) connects through shared digital infrastructure while each business maintains its own brand.

## What We Are Building

| Layer | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| B2B infrastructure | Branded sites, CRM, booking, calendar, payments, staff, analytics, documents |
| B2C experience | Discovery, comparison, booking, payments, document upload, order tracking |
| Marketplace | Supplier catalogs: coffins, urns, flowers, monuments, accessories |
| Cemetery module | Plot management, ceremony scheduling, digitized workflows |
| Platform admin | Tenant lifecycle, billing, support, audit |

## North Star

**Completed digital journeys** — end-to-end cases where discovery, booking, payment, and communication happen on-platform without phone/paper fallback.

## Non-Goals (Explicit)

- Generic no-code website builder for arbitrary industries
- Direct competition with consumer-only memorial sites (without B2B ops layer)
- Full legal/regulatory automation for every EU jurisdiction in MVP
- In-house payment processing (we integrate, not become a bank)

## Principles

1. **Multi-tenant first** — every feature assumes tenant isolation
2. **Industry-specific** — workflows match funeral/cemetery reality, not generic booking
3. **White-label by default** — Memora brand invisible to end customers where configured
4. **Monetization-agnostic architecture** — revenue models are plugins, not foundations
5. **EU compliance by design** — GDPR, data residency, local language
